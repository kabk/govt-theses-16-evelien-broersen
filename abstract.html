Abstract

Copyright is outdated or even should have never existed in the form as we know it. It is completely focused on the designer and not on the user of an existing work.
The exempt of a work leads to development. So what restrictions do alternatives for copyright like anticopyright, copyleft, General Public License and Creative Commons offer and what would be most beneficial for a design and it's future development?

A good work needs authenticity. Genuineness, credibility and originality result in an authentic design. When a work is copied, differences will reveal the authenticity of the user. When will this be enough for a good design?
In history many designers and artists have proven copying can yield very interesting works. It's the intention of the user that counts.

It's not realistic and it will be counterproductive to have a universal idea about what plagiarism and originality mean. These concepts will be filled in differently by each individual.
A combination of licenses like Indymedia Romania and General Public License would be my ideal. A restriction to prevent commercial benefit to the user, requiring publishing research and a restriction to prevent future discrimination against certain groups. Next to these restrictions works should be freely available. Whether attribution of the author is necessary would be up to the user.

Research-question:
What are the consequences of the alternatives for copyright and what is most beneficial for a design?
